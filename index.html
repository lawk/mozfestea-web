About MozFest

MozFest east Africa is a huge event that will bring together educators, innovators, learners and makers from East Africa and beyond to share experiences and build the web together laying a foundation for growth of the internet and information of technology in Africa. This will be an annual event that will later spread across Africa helping address the technological challenges faced in the African continent through meaningful collaborations.

We will have the opportunity to show case technological innovations, brainstorm ways to advance them and also give birth to new ideas.

MozFestEA will be a mix of teaching, making, coding, sharing, learning, hacking and celebrating. Join us for two exhilarating days of forging the future of the web

The Story

Mozilla Festival East Africa (codenamed MozfestEA) is an event based on the annual Mozilla Festival that usually happens in London. It is to be held on 11th – 13th July, 2014 in Kampala, Uganda. The event is organized by the Mozilla Uganda community which represents Mozilla and is also part of the global Hive Learning Network. We’ll have Mark Surman, the Executive Director of the Mozilla Foundation attend on the 13th.

We came up with the concept in Greece and attended the 2013 Mozilla Festival London to see the format and plan for an event which would bring together technology, open source and web enthusiasts, engineers, students, educators, and many others in an effort to see what they’ve done, are doing or are planning to do on or for the open Web.

In Africa, Internet access is picking up but it’s still at the low. The web could do so much for people here, imagine if someone could build affordable technology that would allow someone in a village to access Internet with less hassle, we could teach some mentors who would teach such people how to access educational material online and maybe improve the literacy levels, Communication, Tolerance, Sharing and so much more. All this on an open web and maybe using open hardware.

So far we’ve partnered with Mozilla Developer Network and Webmaker. There are other partnerships in progress like Github, Pepsi Uganda, NTV (media house) and many others.
